### What I actually do

I'm a product manager who got tired of being one step removed from the thing I was managing. So alongside the day job, I ship small, real, open-source tools — the kind that automate the "paste it into an AI chat and ask nicely" tasks people repeat every week without ever fixing.

No demo-ware. Everything here runs, has tests, and does the thing it says on the tin.

### The habit: one small ship, every week

Every week, I pick one genuinely boring, repeated task — something you'd normally hand to ChatGPT or Claude by hand — and build the tool that replaces the ritual. Small scope, real usage, MIT licensed, public from day one.

| Repo | What it kills |
|---|---|
| [`changelog-genie`](https://github.com/us702/changelog-genie) | The "paste `git log` into an AI chat for release notes" ritual — zero-dep CLI, optional Claude polish |
| [`contact-dedupe`](https://github.com/us702/contact-dedupe) | The "paste my messy contact CSV into ChatGPT to clean it up" ritual — matches, merges, and audits |

More ship every Sunday.

### The flagship: a PM-curated AI toolkit

[`awesome-ai-pm-toolkit`](https://github.com/us702/awesome-ai-pm-toolkit) — not another engineer-facing "awesome list." A curated, opinionated map of AI tools and workflows judged from the PM side: can you get from idea to working prototype yourself, today? Every entry gets a PM-fit rating and an honest take, not scraped marketing copy.

### Why this, as a PM

Most PM output is a document that describes a decision someone else has to build. I think the bar for AI-era product management is higher than that — you should be able to prototype the thing you're proposing, not just diagram it. This repo is where I hold myself to that.

### Currently exploring

- Small, single-purpose CLI tools over heavyweight SaaS for internal/personal automation
